Butter chicken serves as a cornerstone of the worlds love for indian cuisine. Enjoyed by many across the globe you'll be hard pressed to find an indian restaurant that doesn't serve this iconic dish. As it's so well loved, many have taken to making this dish at home. This is my favorite recipe to date. Its cheap, easy to make and still delicious.

The Origins Of Butter Chicken

Butter chicken originated from Northern India in 1948. Created by Kundan Lal Gujral, you may notice that Butter Chicken (known as murgh makhani in Hindi â€” which translates to chicken with butter), is similar to the British national dish chicken tikka masala.

This dish is prepared in a buttery gravy and the addition of cream gives the curry sauce a silky smooth rich texture. Most restaurants are known to add in copious amounts of butter, which as a result can leave you feeling bloated or ill from the slick of grease.

However, while recipe testing I found that replacing the butter for sauce with Ghee results in a lighter less rich sauce.

Adding cream at the end of cooking provides enough of that richness you look for in an authentic butter chicken.

How Do We Make Butter Chicken

Butter chicken when compared to other Indian curries is a milder curry. You can add as much or as little chili as you wish. YOU are in total control of how spicy the dish is.

To make the tender, juicy and flavour infused chicken we need to make an easy yoghurt marinade using: plain yoghurt, garam masala and salt.

When it comes to the protein in this dish I prefer bone-in chicken thighs. Any pieces of chicken will do but typically dark meat works better for this recipe.

Step by Step

  • First, mix your yogurt marinade together.
  • Marinate chicken for 20 minutes, or overnight is preferable. (The longer it marinates, the more tender your chicken will be.)
  • Brown your chicken in a pan. Keep the charred brown bits from the chicken in the pan. We call the charred bits fond. Fond adds flavour to any dish. If the fond in your pan is burnt too much don't use it as burnt fond makes the dish bitter.
  • Make your curry sauce in the same pan you browned the chicken in and let simmer.
  • Finally add the chicken to the sauce and let simmer.

Vegan or Vegetarian Replacements

The butter in this recipe can be replaced with margarine or an equal amount of vegetable oil.

Instead of heavy cream for this recipe you can use coconut cream for a vegan replacement.

And lastly maybe the most important part of the dish, the chicken. The chicken can be replaced with tofu thats been marinated in the same yoghurt marinade meant for the chicken.

BUTTER CHICKEN

Butter Chicken is creamy and easy to make right at home in one pan with simple ingredients! It's full of incredible flavours, that rivals any dish! Aromatic golden chicken pieces in an incredible creamy curry sauce, this Butter Chicken recipe is one of my personal favorites!

Prep:

40 mins

Cook:

30 mins

Total:

1 hr 10 mins

INGREDIENTS:

Chicken:

  • 1 cup (240g) full-fat yoghurt
  • 1 Tbsp (15g) garam masala
  • 1 Tbsp (15g) salt
  • 800g of Chicken

Butter Chicken Curry:

  • 2 Tbsp (30g) vegetable oil
  • 1 large yellow onion diced
  • 2-inch (5cm) knob of ginger, finely diced
  • 6 cloves of sliced garlic
  • 2 tsp (8g) paprika
  • 2 tsp (9g) ground cumin
  • 1 Tbsp (14g) garam masala
  • 1 1/2 tsp (6g) turmeric powder(optional)
  • 1 can (400g) of crushed tomatoes
  • 2/3 cup (160ml) water
  • 1 cup (250ml) heavy cream
  • 2 Tbsp (28g) unsalted butter
  • Steamed rice
  • Fresh Coriander

INSTRUCTIONS:

Method:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together yoghurt, garam masala, and salt.
  2. Toss with sliced chicken thighs until fully coated. Cover and marinate for 30 minutes or overnight.
  3. In a large pan, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat until shimmering hot.
  4. Add chicken in batches and sear 2-3 minutes on each side. Set to side once done.
  5. Lower the same pan to medium heat, add a touch of more oil along with diced onions, diced ginger, sliced garlic, and season to taste with salt and pepper. Mix and stir, sauteing for 2-3 minutes or until softened.
  6. Add in spices (turmeric powder optional) and stir together until spices have toasted for 1-2 minutes.
  7. After toasting, add in crushed tomatoes and water then stir until incorporated over medium heat. Let simmer for 5-8 minutes until the sauce has reduced.
  8. Once reduced, add chicken back to the pan mixing into the sauce and let simmer for 3-5 minutes.
  9. Add in heavy cream stirring until thickened and incorporated. Bring up to a light simmer before reducing heat to low and simmering for no longer than 3-4 minutes. Stir often.
  10. Turn off the heat and continuously stir in unsalted butter until melted. Season to taste with salt.
  11. Plate in a shallow bowl with steam rice and topped with cilantro.
